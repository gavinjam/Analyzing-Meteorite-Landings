Introduction

This website displays the results of analyzing meteorite landings. Python libraries was used to do a Numerical Analysis, Graphical Analysis, and as well as train a ML model to predict the location of a meteorite landing. The data is from a Nasa's open source database of datasets. Click this link to view it.



Motivation and Background

Understanding meteorite landings can help us understand how Earth and the Universe were created. Meteorites also contain precious materials that can tell the timeline of a meteorite in correlation to Earth. It has an economic bonus as well because we get to understand how these materials can be used and can help us find places to mine for resources when Earth’s metals become scarce. Some of these calculations can help us understand meteorites in the future and may help answer questions like how meteorites caused the extinction of dinosaurs. Also, Nasa’s visualizations currently for this dataset are not that great for the general public to understand and some of the current visualizations do not create graphs correctly.



Research Questions

1. What is the most popular and most rare meteorite class that has landed on Earth and where specifically?

2. What is the average mass, range of mass, the biggest mass, and smallest mass of meteorites that have landed on Earth?

3. Can we visually see where meteorites landed in different places of the world and analyze how big they were?

4. Can we predict where a meteorite is most likely going to hit Earth based on the class type, mass, and year?
